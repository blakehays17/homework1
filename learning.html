The Mozilla Developer Network is without-a-doubt the best resource for learning HTML. It has every definition related to every code used in HTML. By consistently returning to the MDN I began to not just learn, but know what each elements' purpose was in HTML.

uDemy is a great online learning platform for nearly anything you could imagine. I simply searched "HTML and CSS" and stumbled accross the course, "Web Bootcamp 2022." This encouraged me to dig deeper into HTML and CSS as I worked out practice problems on my own and took notes on all the minute details.

Finally, I had enough knowledge to write my own code. I started out by simply writing headers, paragraphs, and line breaks. I then figured out how to insert images and tables into HTML which helped me obtain a deeper understanding. Eventually, after numerous hours, I am able to present you with this website.
